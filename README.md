## 📋 Overview

<a href="#photoMechanic">Photo Mechanic</a> serves professional photographers whose primary challenge with post-production is throughput — photographers who shoot in volume and need to process, select, and deliver images faster than integrated editing and culling tools allow.

The <a href="#photoMechanic">photojournalism use case</a> is Photo Mechanic's original and strongest market — news photographers who shoot thousands of frames under deadline and need to identify the selectable images, add captions, credits, and location metadata, and transmit to editorial systems in the minimum possible time. Photo Mechanic's speed is the tool that makes same-day editorial turnaround practical for high-volume event coverage. The <a href="#photoMechanic">wedding photography workflow</a> uses Photo Mechanic for first-pass culling of wedding shoots that regularly produce two to four thousand RAW files per event — identifying and rating the selects from the full shoot as a first step before importing only those selects into Lightroom for editing. This prevents Lightroom from importing and building previews for thousands of images that will be deleted, dramatically reducing the time Lightroom spends on each wedding.

The <a href="#photoMechanic">sports and action photography culling</a> handles the high frame rates that burst shooting produces — motor drive sequences of ten to twenty frames of the same moment require rapid review to identify the peak moment, and Photo Mechanic's instant preview enables this review at the frame rate the shoot requires. <a href="#photoMechanic">Commercial photographers</a> use Photo Mechanic's metadata workflow for embedding usage rights, model releases, copyright, and client information into images at the point of delivery rather than after editing.

<a href="#photoMechanic">Variables system</a> in metadata templates uses smart variables like {date}, {time}, {camera}, and {sequence} in naming templates and metadata fields — enabling consistent automated metadata and naming across shoots without manual entry for each field that can be derived from image data. <a href="#photoMechanic">Transmit integration</a> sends images directly from Photo Mechanic to FTP servers for news wire transmission — the photojournalism deadline workflow where images need to be captioned and transmitted within minutes of capture.

<a href="#photoMechanic">Photo Mechanic Plus</a> adds a DAM (digital asset management) database component to the core Photo Mechanic speed with a catalog system for organizing and searching across an entire photo library — combining the speed of Photo Mechanic with library management for photographers who want both capabilities. <a href="#photoMechanic">Industry-standard workflow position</a> — Photo Mechanic is used by the majority of professional news photographers worldwide and is a standard tool at major sports events, news organizations, and commercial photography studios.
